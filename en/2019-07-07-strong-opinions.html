“Территория заблуждений” с Игорем Прокопенко на Рен-ТВ — с недавних пор моя любимая телепередача. В ней нет ни здравого смысла, ни проверенных фактов, ни объективных взглядов, ни единой попытки трезвого анализа. Именно для этого я ее и смотрю: чтобы точно понимать, во что именно хочет власть, чтобы я верил.

I like the work of Prokopenko. He does not get distracted by the opinions of opponents, does not burden the presentation with facts, does not clutter the airwaves with quotes and the names of real experts, does not delve into details and specifics. He speaks not to the listener’s mind, but to his heart. He generalizes and simplifies: “Western theorists,” “world experts,” “European leaders,” and “British scientists”. He clearly and concisely divides the world into “our engineers” and “Russian soldiers,” “American soldiers” and “European homosexuals,” “Rothschilds” and “Rockefellers.” He is very easy to understand, his words do not require a second thought. He presents vividly and convincingly.

It is interesting that he does not always criticize the West and praise Russia. At first, this is surprising, as all his unequivocal rhetoric seems to be pure shameless propaganda of Russia of Putin at first glance. However, very soon you begin to understand that it is not always the case.

In one plot, he has idiotic American generals commanding lazy, obese soldiers, while in another, these malicious and dangerous villains use any opportunity to ruin our lives. In one plot, the whole of Georgia is populated by Nazis, while in another, they are a fraternal nation that needs help. In one, Poroshenko is a criminal, while in another, he is a victim. In one, Russia thrives, while in another, its population dies from diseases, hunger, and rampant corruption.

After several hours of viewing this info-trash, you begin to understand what exactly they want from you. It’s not about love for Putin, or hatred for Trump, or sympathy for Georgian children who fell into the hands of Nazis, or belief in the inviolability of our borders. Not at all. They expect us to be ready to have simple and categorical judgments on the most complex and important issues.

When I hear for the first time the phrase “world experts have confirmed,” I want to ask, which experts exactly are being referred to? When I hear it for the second time a few minutes later, I smile and understand that they don’t intend to present me with any experts. When I hear it for the tenth time, I realize that there are no experts, there never were and never will be. And they are not needed anyway. What they want is for me to stop looking for experts, to stop analyzing, to stop doubting and relying on logic. They want me to stop thinking and start judging and condemning.

When I started this political blog several years ago, I had an old acquaintance who used to read me on Facebook. She read and commented on my first articles and always asked me, who exactly do I support? She expected me to take a clear position, especially on the issues of Crimea and Donbass, Putin and Ukraine, the Russian and Ukrainian languages. She wanted to know exactly whose side I was on. However, I urged her not to seek categorical and unambiguous truth, but to see contradictions and conflicts between different equally interesting points of view. In the end, she deleted me as a friend, saying literally, “Your blog is empty!”

Unlike my blog, Igor Prokopenko’s shows on Ren-TV are not empty. They are filled to the brim with absolute truths. And it doesn’t matter on whose side these truths are. What matters is that they help the viewer calm down, stop hesitating, stop worrying, exhale, and take one of the proposed positions: either for or against.

Propaganda of the first level calls for: “For the Motherland, for Stalin!” This propaganda seems to be significantly weaker than the propaganda of the second level: “For!”

Just a “Yes!” without clarification. Prokopenko and others like him will talk about the war, the Maidan, the Nazis, and LGBT festivals in their next TV broadcasts. Our task is to listen to them, ignore the absence and distortion of facts, manipulation and incomplete statistics, exaggerations and blatant distortion of opinions, and to find for ourselves what will fill our lives with meaning: the clarity of conclusions.

If Goebbels, the first major master of propaganda, needed a population convinced of the inevitable victory of National Socialism, then modern propagandists need a population that can be persuaded. To achieve this, we need to learn how to adopt one of two positions and stick to it until the end.

Many years ago, I had a dispute with a comrade, who was quite educated and a believer in Judaism. He argued that in the Arab-Israeli conflict, the Arabs alone were to blame, as they hindered the development of the young state of Israel. On the other hand, I tried to show him that both sides of the conflict had their own truth, and the Arab world also had valid claims. For some time, we passionately exchanged facts, and then he fell silent, looked me in the eye and asked, “Whose side are you on, anyway?”

Listening to arguments from both sides, weighing opinions, asking questions, seeking answers, doubting, and admitting one’s mistakes - is much more difficult than knowing for certain whose side is right. Modern propaganda plays on these weaknesses of ours and exploits them. It no longer explains why national-socialism or communism is the highest form of social organization. It simply teaches us to vote for one of the proposed options. And to do it quickly, emotionally, and wholeheartedly. It teaches us to seek enemies and friends without wasting time on intermediate stages of analysis and fact-checking.

It is much easier to govern a population trained in this way than those who do not rush to give their vote for one of the “truths” but are used to seeking the truth somewhere in between.

Paraphrasing my Facebook acquaintance, it can be concluded that a modern “empty” person is someone who lacks a clear opinion on any controversial political issue. Doubts are merely obstacles on the path to a bright future!
