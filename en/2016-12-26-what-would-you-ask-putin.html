And what would you ask Putin if you were at the microphone?

I anticipate your options. When will you return Crimea, scoundrel? When will the sanctions be lifted? When will the political prisoners be released? When will the stealing in Russia stop? And in general, how long will we tolerate all of this?

I want to disappoint you - Putin has calm and logical answers to all these seemingly acute questions. He is not afraid of these questions, and “brave” journalists ask him that. I put the word “brave” in quotes because, in my opinion, it doesn’t take much courage to ask Putin when he will return Crimea. He didn’t take it. We have a criminal code and courts where no one hides anything from anyone. If the investigation has evidence, the accused receives what they deserve. If not, they are released. Such questions are meaningless. By asking them, we only demonstrate our lack of understanding of the situation and our silent agreement with it. And the lack of understanding lies in the fact that we are looking for a king or defender of our serf-like interests in the president, rather than a temporarily elected and hired executor of our will.
