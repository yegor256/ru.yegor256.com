“Кому нужна война”

Externally, all wars are the same: flags, logos, heroes, cripples, and victory marches. In all wars, people die and calls for unity for the sake of victory over the enemy, for the defense of the homeland are heard. Every time, soldiers on the opposite side of the field, city, or trench are called the enemy, and the homeland is the place where fire coordination is conducted. But are these soldiers really our real enemies, and is the homeland exactly there?

Imagine that you are the owner of a company that produces equipment for metallurgical plants, and I am the owner of one of those plants. You live and work in Kharkiv, and my plant is in Donetsk. You make equipment for me, and I buy it from you. With the help of your electronics, I produce and sell metal sheets and reinforcement. Tell me, why would I want to fight with you?

Moreover, why should I defend myself against you? What motives do we have to arm ourselves and blow each other up? What economic sense does it make? Work is in progress, enterprises are functioning, people are getting paid, production capacities are growing, profits are increasing, new technologies are being implemented. Why should we fight?

Any war, except for economic and competitive ones, is not needed by us, producers of goods and services. Moreover, it only harms us.

Кому же она нужна?

Лозунг защиты отечества есть сплошь да рядом обыва­тельски-несознательное оправдание войны Ленин

The thing is, the proposed picture is missing one important element - the state. They won’t let us work and trade peacefully. States are forming around us. They will convince us that we are potential enemies to each other, that all you think about is how to rob me, while I dream of seeing your factory in ruins. They will call me Ukrainian and you Russian. I will have a yellow-blue flag, and you a tricolor one. I will have my own national history, and you will have yours. And in order to deliver my goods to you, I will have to go through border control and pay customs. They will divide us and enclose us with a fence.

To those who do this, namely our states, war will be necessary.

War is necessary for the state for self-preservation. In most cases, its main enemy is its own people (there are exceptions, but they are rare). In order for us to continue working for the benefit of officials from the state apparatus, we need to constantly convince ourselves of the value of this apparatus. Otherwise, sooner or later, you and I will sit in two bulldozers and tear down this fence that has been built between us, along with the customs and the checkpoint.

To prevent this from happening, to avoid riots and revolutions, it is necessary to support and strengthen the value of the concept of “Motherland”. We all must understand that this land is “ours”, but beyond this fence, a kilometer away, it is already “not ours”. And since the Motherland is so valuable, it must be defended. An army is needed, war is needed, heroes are needed, sacrifices are needed, monuments are needed, memorials are needed, military-patriotic events are needed. And, of course, one must pay for the defense of the Motherland.

War is needed by officials and not needed by us. If we understand this, then we will stop celebrating heroes, stop laying flowers at their monuments, stop hanging national flags on the facades of buildings and windshields. We will understand that both the flag, the anthem, patriotism, and national consciousness are tools of manipulation invented by states against their people.

The more these tools are visible and the more the state talks about patriotism, the more strained its relationship with its own people becomes. The less trust it deserves.
