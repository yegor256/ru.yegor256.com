“Зачем?”

Recently, one of the readers of my political essays passionately asked me why I do this? What am I trying to achieve and why am I writing? What am I calling for? Whom am I for and with whom? What actions do I expect from the readers? He was also very interested in the question of where my homeland really is: in Moscow or in Kiev? The answer seemed to surprise and disappoint him.

Recently, I read a new book by Eduard Limonov, “Azbuka” - a collection of thoughts on various topics, almost unrelated to each other - about politics, the Donbass, Putin, old age, violence, and of course, women. The text is juicy and frank, and the ideas are radical and sound, for example:

We are constantly being pushed into the shit of revolution, claiming that the number of victims of the revolution testifies in favor of evolution. But has anyone bothered to count the number of victims of evolution? All those who died from alcoholism, suicides, depression? How many children were not born out of the despair and hopelessness of the previous generation? Now they have forced us to live by evolution. I know thousands of people who couldn’t realize their talents. And they are rotting away on their feet.

Или вот это:

The people do not change the authorities. The people are unable to change anything. They are scattered, attached to home, to children, to a conservative and apolitical wife (most women are apolitical, and under an authoritarian regime, life is better and safer for them, the weaker sex).

Однако, при том, что я согласен с автором почти везде, меня удивило его наивное недоумение:

Square-headed liberals chatter that I supposedly support Putin. But I have more disagreements with Putin than all of their stupid and deceitful liberal clique.

Is it really for you, Eduard Veniaminovich, with a prison sentence and a war behind you, not to know how the consciousness of the masses works? Our consciousness. You provoke reflections and reasoning, while we wait for conclusions. You question basic truths, and it makes us uncomfortable. You offer analysis, while we wait for synthesis. For us, square-headed people, it is very important to know: Do you actually support Putin or not?!

Give us conclusions, directives, precise algorithms. Synthesize clarity for us, and we will follow you. Divide the world in half and tell us under whose banners we should stand. However, you refuse to do it, you find it repulsive, quoting:

Вы хотите, чтобы все было “за” или “против”? Обратитесь к КПРФ. Это они такие тупые.

Вы отказываетесь тупеть вместе с нами, а мы отказываемся Вас поддерживать. Однако, так было не всегда.

Previous generations witnessed an open war between two ideologies. The newspaper “Pravda” wrote the truth, while the magazine Time lied. And vice versa, depending on who was reading. It doesn’t matter who was actually right. What matters is that objectively the bipolar agenda gathered the intellectual elite around it. Where else should they gather, if the world is polarized and it’s certainly interesting to talk and think about it?! The first books by Limonov were written from emigration, where he ended up after a conflict with the KGB - “we will either imprison you or you leave,” as he himself put it.

The vector of social conflict (capitalism versus communism) coincided with the vector of the most progressive thought (freedom or slavery). The best people were either in the academy of sciences or in exile. And in both places, there were geniuses, heated debates, and the opportunity to find the truth.

However, the ideology of Lenin and Stalin has lost. Limonov returned to Russia. He returned to a place where the war had already ended. There were no longer two camps, no dissidents, and no KGB - the fire of the world revolution had died down. The intellectual elite returned from exile and celebrated victory, while the economy surrendered to the mercy of the victors - they brought us McDonald’s and Casio calculators, in exchange for oil and a hungry and illiterate market of 150 million people. We emerged from the dark interlude of the 90s without a new political agenda. The Soviet Union, once the “empire of evil,” turned into a handful of underdeveloped colonies, no longer thinking about fighting the yoke of capital, and people became weaker and more cowardly. There was no longer a need to search for truth - work for Uncle Sam and be happy.

Nevertheless, intellectuals with their eternal protest needed to be directed somewhere. We needed that very bipolarity that we lost by dismantling Iron Felix. As a result, intentionally or accidentally, the liberal movement emerged, for everything good and against everything bad. The “concerned intelligentsia” joined it because there was nothing else to join (not to the bloody regime of VVP!).

However, despite the simplified formula “down with Putin!” the world is no longer as simple as it was in Limonov’s youth. It is no longer divided into Uncle Sam and the proletariat, and therefore, just by removing Putin and Co. from power, it is unlikely that all the problems can be solved as they were once, by instantly abandoning all the achievements of the October Revolution. I am convinced that liberals, unlike their thousands of followers, understand this, but they cannot do anything about it. In order for others to follow them, they need to shout loudly and clearly: “Down with…” and then a specific name.

It turns out to be really loud, but there is little common sense in this cry. Therefore, only those who are not used to searching for meaning gather under the banners… and bored passersby. As a result, three camps are formed instead of two, as before: 1) the authorities, 2) liberals, and 3) the thinking minority. There are no banners over the third camp, and no one is arrested there, and its population is constantly decreasing. It is precisely there that you can find people like Limonov - dying veterans of the great war.

Once, in one of the restaurants in Moscow, I was asked to fill out a form to get a regular visitor card. The last item of the simple questionnaire was a sarcastic question “Whose Crimea?” with a free field to fill in. It took me a few minutes to come up with a concise response, different from primitive “ours” or “not ours”.

The world has become more complex, simple formulas like “ours vs. yours” or “freedom vs. slavery” are no longer able to describe the reality that surrounds us. Anyone who wants to present a situation in terms of “for” or “against” is not just a fool. They are harmful. Whether they are liberals or Kremlin propagandists.

Why am I writing this blog? To instill in you, my readers, a love for forms with free fields. If we learn to fill in the free fields, there will be true freedom.
