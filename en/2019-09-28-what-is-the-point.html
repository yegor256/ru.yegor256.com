“Зачем?”

Recently, one of the readers of my political essays passionately asked me why I do this? What am I trying to achieve and why am I writing? What am I calling for? Whom am I for and with whom? What actions do I expect from the readers? He was also very interested in the question of where my homeland really is: in Moscow or in Kiev? The answer seemed to surprise and disappoint him.

Not long ago, I read a new book by Eduard Limonov called “Azbuka” (https://www.litres.ru/eduard-limonov/azbuka-imperator-i-drugie-mneniya/chitat-onlayn/) — a collection of thoughts on various topics, almost unrelated to each other — about politics, Donbass, Putin, old age, violence, and of course, women. The text is juicy and candid, and the ideas are radical and sensible, for example:

We are constantly being pushed into the shit of revolution, claiming that the number of victims of revolution supports evolution. But has anyone bothered to count the number of victims of evolution? All those who have died from alcoholism, suicide, depression? How many children were not born out of the despair and hopelessness of the previous generation? Now they have forced us to live according to evolution. I know thousands of people who have been unable to fulfill their talents. And they are rotting away on their feet.

Или вот это:

People do not change power. People are not capable of changing anything. They are scattered, attached to their home, children, conservative and apolitical wife (the majority of women are apolitical, moreover, under an authoritarian regime, life is better and safer for them - the weaker sex).

However, while I agree with the author almost everywhere, I was surprised by his naive bewilderment:

Square-headed liberals chatter that I supposedly support Putin. Well, I have more disagreements with Putin than all of their stupid and deceitful liberal entourage.

Do you, Eduard Veniaminovich, with prison and war behind you, not know how the consciousness of the masses works? Our consciousness. You provoke thoughts and reasoning, while we await conclusions. You question elementary truths, which makes us uncomfortable. You offer analysis, while we expect synthesis. For us, squareheads, it is very important to know: Do you actually support Putin or not?!

Give us conclusions, directives, precise algorithms. Synthesize clarity for us, and we will follow you. Divide the world in half and tell us under whose banners we should stand. However, you refuse to do this, it disgusts you, I quote:

Do you want everything to be “for” or “against”? Refer to the Communist Party of the Russian Federation. They are so stupid.

You refuse to be dull with us, and we refuse to support you. However, it was not always like this.

Previous generations witnessed an open war between two ideologies. The newspaper “Pravda” told the truth, while Time magazine lied. However, it depended on who was reading. It doesn’t matter who was actually right. What matters is that objectively the bipolar agenda gathered the intellectual elite around it. Where else could they gather if the world is polarized, and it is certainly interesting to talk and think about it?! Limonov’s first books were written from emigration, where he ended up after a conflict with the KGB — “either we’ll imprison you or you leave,” as he himself put it.

The vector of social conflict (capitalism versus communism) coincided with the vector of the most progressive thought (freedom or slavery). The best people were either in the academy of sciences or in exile. Both there and there were geniuses, heated debates, and the opportunity to find the truth.

However, the ideology of Lenin and Stalin has lost. Limonov returned to Russia. He returned to a place where the war had already ended. There were no longer two camps, no dissidents, and no KGB - the fire of world revolution had died down. The intellectual elite returned from exile and celebrated victory, while the economy surrendered to the mercy of the victors - they brought us McDonald’s and Casio calculators in exchange for oil and a 150 million hungry and illiterate market. We emerged from the grim interlude of the 90s without a new political agenda. The Soviet Union, once known as the “empire of evil,” turned into a handful of underdeveloped colonies that no longer dreamed of fighting the yoke of capital, and people became weaker and more cowardly. There was no longer a need to seek the truth - work for Uncle Sam and be happy.

Nevertheless, intellectuals with their eternal protest needed to be directed somewhere. We needed that very bipolarity that we lost by dismantling Iron Felix. As a result, intentionally or accidentally, the liberal movement emerged, for everything good and against everything bad. The “concerned intelligentsia” joined it, as there was nothing else to join (certainly not the bloody regime of VVP!).

However, despite the simplified formula of “down with Putin!” the world is no longer as simple as in Limonov’s youth. It is no longer divided into Uncle Sam and the proletariat, and therefore, only by removing Putin and Co. from power, it is unlikely to solve all the problems as it used to be, by abandoning all the achievements of the October Revolution. I am convinced that liberals, unlike their thousands of followers, understand this, but they can’t do anything about it. In order for others to follow them, they need to shout loudly and distinctly: “Down with…” and then a specific name.

It turns out to be really loud, but there is little common sense in this cry. Therefore, only those who are not used to searching for meaning and … bored passers-by gather under the banners. As a result, three camps are formed instead of two, as before: 1) the authorities, 2) liberals, and 3) the thinking minority. There are no flags over the third camp, and no one is arrested there, and its numbers are constantly decreasing. It is precisely there that you can find people like Limonov - dying veterans of the Great War.

Once, in one of the restaurants in Moscow, I was asked to fill out a form to get a membership card. The last item on the simple questionnaire was a sarcastic question “Whose Crimea?” with a blank field for filling in. It took me a few minutes to come up with a concise response, different from primitive “ours” or “not ours”.

The world has become more complex, simple formulas like “ours vs. yours” or “freedom vs. slavery” are no longer able to describe the reality surrounding us. Anyone who wants to present a situation in terms of “for” or “against” is not just foolish. They are harmful. Whether they are liberals or Kremlin propagandists.

Why am I writing this blog? To instill in you, my readers, a love for forms with free fields. If we can fill in the free fields, then we will have true freedom.
