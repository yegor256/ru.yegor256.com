“Государство как услуга. Почему нет? Потому что.”

Historically, states were needed, among other things, for more effective conduct of wars. The larger the group, the easier it was for them to defeat smaller aggressors, the easier it was to capture adjacent territories. In order to conduct war more efficiently, technologies, science, education, culture, medicine, and much more were needed. However, all of this could be done without the establishment of states and the imposition of rigid borders between them. Countries, flags, anthems, borders, and national interests are necessary where there is war. And there is war everywhere and always.

However, the world is changing, and technologies are advancing. First and foremost, communication and labor automation technologies. Thanks to the Internet and robots, physical presence in the place of creating value-added products is becoming less important for an increasing number of professions. Programmers, of course, first and foremost. Twenty years ago, it was very important for them to be close to the product they were creating because the product was on expensive servers, and telephone or email communication alone was not enough for effective work. Now the servers have moved to the “cloud,” and communication has become not less, but often more efficient through video conferences or online chats.

Architects, designers, engineers, musicians, bloggers, writers, journalists, artists, managers, marketers, copywriters, producers, investors, analysts, and many other professions that are not tied to the land and do not require manual labor, now often have the freedom to choose their place of residence and even country. Why live where you were born if you can live where it’s warmer? Why pay a high income tax in France if you can pay a low or zero tax in Dubai? Why rent an apartment in San Francisco for $5,000 if you can write the same articles for the California Weekly while renting a bungalow in Bali for $500?

You can broaden the question even further, as Pavel Cherkashin did in his recent article The homeland-as-a-service model: How blockchain will disrupt the world order (Russian version here): why not turn the state into a service and its citizens into clients? Everyone will be free to decide in which state they want to live by choosing from all the available offers on the market.

Let’s suppose, Belgium offers quality healthcare, but has high taxes. In Brazil, taxes are lower, but the crime situation is tense. In the US, taxes are acceptable and the crime rate is not as high, but public transportation and roads are of low quality. And so on. Each country will have something to offer its clients. Something like office rentals or trendy coworking spaces - each has its own conditions, price, advantages, and disadvantages.

Pavel Cherkashin predicts in his article that very soon “states will have to compete for the most qualified citizens by providing the best services and such advantages as a strong economy, low taxes, a fair legal system, and reliable social security programs”.

Sounds beautiful! Isn’t it a utopia?

Да, она.

The authors of such ideas about free migration, carried away, forget why states are needed in the first place. And they are needed, as mentioned above, for conducting conquering and liberating wars between people. Despite the fact that a small percentage of the planet’s inhabitants enjoy studying new technologies and writing sensible articles about them (including myself), the overwhelming majority fight for survival in a semi-war or war mode.

According to the World Bank, half of the world’s population lives below the poverty line, according to information. The dynamics of social inequality, according to a recent report from the World Inequality Lab, are bleak. The gap between the poor and the rich is widening: “the share of national income going to the top 10% of earners is 37% in Europe, 41% in China, 46% in Russia, 47% in the US and Canada, and about 55% in sub-Saharan Africa, Brazil, and India, and 61% in the Middle East.”

Will all these people who live on a few dollars a month be offered to choose a country of residence? They have already chosen. The USA. And now specifically for them, Donald Trump is building a wall. So that they cannot realize their choice.

The USA, like most countries in Western Europe, is in a state of escalating semi-military conflict with countries where there is less money but no less desire to live well. The flow of emigration from the Middle East to Europe is rapidly increasing. This is not yet a third world war, but it is also not the time to talk about abolishing visas, passports, opening borders, and eliminating immigration control.

States are still needed and still serve military purposes.

Wars have only changed their form, but they have not disappeared anywhere.

Perhaps, we can talk about the “state as a service”, but with one important caveat: it will be a very expensive service, and only for a very select few. However, such a service already exists in the market, without any blockchains. Want to live and work in the USA - the EB-5 visa for $1,000,000 is always available. Want to live in Zurich, 300 thousand francs a year, and welcome. And so on. The borders have long been open. But only for the wealthy.

Will they ever be open to everyone? I don’t think so. Will a child born in the slums of Bombay, who reaches the age of 18 and decides that it is better to be a citizen of the United States, live in Miami, work there as a programmer, and pay taxes there, be able to press a couple of buttons and obtain an American passport? No, this will never or almost never happen.

Unfortunately or fortunately, it’s hard to say.
