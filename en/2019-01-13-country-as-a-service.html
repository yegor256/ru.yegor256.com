“Государство как услуга. Почему нет? Потому что.”

Historically, states were necessary, among other things, for more effective conduct of wars. The larger the group, the easier it was for them to defeat smaller aggressors, the easier it was to annex adjacent territories. In order to conduct war more effectively, technologies, science, education, culture, medicine, and much more were needed. However, all of this could be done without the formation of states and the establishment of rigid borders between them. Countries, flags, anthems, borders, and national interests are needed where there is war. And war is everywhere and always.

However, the world is changing and technologies are evolving. First of all, communication and labor automation technologies. Thanks to the Internet and robots, physical presence in the place of creating value-added products is becoming less important for an increasing number of professions. Programmers, of course, primarily. Twenty years ago, it was very important for them to be close to the product they create because the product was located on expensive servers, and mere telephone or email communication was not enough for efficient work. Now the servers have moved to the “cloud”, and communication has become not less, but often more efficient through video conferences or online chats.

Architects, designers, engineers, musicians, bloggers, writers, journalists, artists, managers, marketers, copywriters, producers, investors, analysts, and many other professions that are not tied to the land and do not require manual labor now often have the freedom to choose their place of residence and even the country. Why live where you were born if you can live where it’s warmer? Why pay high income tax in France if you can pay low or zero tax in Dubai? Why rent an apartment in San Francisco for $5,000 if you can write the same articles for the California Weekly while renting a bungalow in Bali for $500?

You can broaden the question even further, as Pavel Cherkashin did in his recent article The homeland-as-a-service model: How blockchain will disrupt the world order (Russian version here): why not turn the state into a service, and its citizens into customers? Everyone will be free to decide in which country they want to live by choosing from all available offers on the market.

Let’s suppose that Belgium offers quality healthcare but has high taxes. In Brazil, taxes are lower, but the crime situation is tense. In the USA, taxes are acceptable and the crime rate is not as high, but public transportation and roads are of low quality. And so on. Each country will have something to offer its clients. Something like office rentals or trendy coworking spaces - each with its own conditions, price, pros, and cons.

Pavel Cherkashin predicts in his article that very soon “states will have to compete for the most qualified citizens by providing the best services and such advantages as a strong economy, low taxes, a fair legal system, and reliable social security programs.”

Authors of such ideas about free migration, getting carried away, forget why states are needed in the first place. And they are needed, as mentioned above, for conducting predatory and liberating wars among people. Despite the fact that a small percentage of the inhabitants of this planet enjoy studying new technologies and writing sensible articles about them (including myself), the overwhelming majority struggle for survival in a semi-war or war mode.

According to the information from the World Bank, half of the world’s population lives below the poverty line. The dynamics of social inequality, according to a recent report from the World Inequality Lab, are disheartening. The gap between the poor and the rich is widening: “the share of national income held by the top 10% earners is 37% in Europe, 41% in China, 46% in Russia, 47% in the United States and Canada, and around 55% in Sub-Saharan Africa, Brazil, and India, and 61% in the Middle East.”

Will all these people living on a few dollars a month be offered to choose a country of residence? They have already chosen. The United States. And now specifically for them, Donald Trump is building a wall. So that they cannot realize their choice.

The United States, like most countries in Western Europe, is in a state of escalating semi-military conflict with countries where there is less money but no less desire to live well. The flow of emigration from the Middle East to Europe is rapidly growing. This is not yet the third world war, but it is also not the time to talk about visa cancellation, passport abolition, border opening, and the elimination of immigration control.

Probably, we can talk about “government as a service”, but with one important caveat: it will be a very expensive service, and only for a very select few. But such a service already exists on the market, without any Blockchains. Want to live and work in the USA - the EB-5 visa for $1,000,000 is always available. Want to live in Zurich, 300 thousand francs a year, and welcome. And so on. The borders have long been open. But only for the wealthy.

Will they ever be open to everyone? I don’t think so. Will a child, born in the slums of Bombay, ever be able to reach the age of 18 and decide that it is better to be a citizen of the United States, live in Miami, work there as a programmer, and pay taxes there - press a couple of buttons and get an American passport?
