“Государство как услуга. Почему нет? Потому что.”

Historically, states were needed, among other things, for more effective warfare. The larger the group, the easier it was for them to defeat smaller aggressors and to conquer adjacent territories. In order to conduct warfare more effectively, technologies, science, education, culture, medicine, and much more were needed. However, all of this could be done without the formation of states and the establishment of rigid borders between them. Countries, flags, anthems, borders, and national interests are necessary where there is war. And war exists everywhere and always.

However, the world is changing and technologies are developing. First and foremost, communication and labor automation technologies. Thanks to the Internet and robots, physical presence in the place of creating value-added products is becoming less important for an increasing number of professions. Programmers, of course, first and foremost. Twenty years ago, it was very important for them to be close to the product they create because the product was located on expensive servers, and telephone or email communication alone was not enough for efficient work. Now servers have moved to the “cloud,” and communication has become not less, but often more efficient through video conferences or online chats.

Architects, designers, planners, musicians, bloggers, writers, journalists, artists, managers, marketers, copywriters, producers, investors, analysts, and many other professions that are not tied to the land and do not require manual labor now often have the freedom to choose their place of residence and even their country. Why live where you were born if you can live where it is warmer? Why pay high income tax in France if you can pay a low or zero tax in Dubai? Why rent an apartment in San Francisco for $5,000 when you can write the same articles for the California Weekly while renting a bungalow in Bali for $500?

You can broaden the question even further, as Pavel Cherkashin did in his recent article “The homeland-as-a-service model: How blockchain will disrupt the world order” (Russian version here): why not turn the state into a service, and its citizens into clients? Everyone will be free to decide in which state they want to live, choosing from all the available offers on the market.

Let’s say Belgium offers quality healthcare, but has high taxes. In Brazil, taxes are lower, but the crime situation is tense. In the USA, taxes are acceptable and the crime rate is not as high, but public transportation and roads are of low quality. And so on. Each country will have something to offer its clients. Something like office rentals or currently popular coworking spaces - each has its own conditions, its own price, its own pros and cons.

Pavel Cherkashin predicts in his article that very soon “states will have to compete for the most qualified citizens by providing the best services and advantages such as a strong economy, low taxes, a fair legal system, and reliable social security programs.”

Sounds beautiful! Isn’t it a utopia?

Да, она.

The authors of such ideas about free migration, carried away, forget why states are needed in the first place. And, as mentioned above, they are needed for conducting aggressive and liberating wars between people. Despite the fact that a small percentage of the inhabitants of this planet enjoy studying new technologies and writing reasonable articles about them (including myself), the overwhelming majority are struggling for survival in a semi-war or war mode.

According to the World Bank, half of the world’s population lives below the poverty line, according to information. The dynamics of social inequality, according to a recent report from the World Inequality Lab, are discouraging. The gap between the poor and the rich is widening: “the share of national income received by the top 10% of earners is 37% in Europe, 41% in China, 46% in Russia, 47% in the United States and Canada, and about 55% in sub-Saharan Africa, Brazil, and India, and 61% in the Middle East” according to the report.

Will all these people who live on a few dollars a month be offered to choose a country to live in? They have already chosen. The USA. And now specifically for them, Donald Trump is building a wall. So that they couldn’t turn their choice into reality.

The United States, like most Western European countries, is in a state of escalating semi-military conflict with countries where there is less money and no desire to live well. The flow of emigration from the Middle East to Europe is rapidly increasing. This is not yet the Third World War, but it is also not a time when one can talk about the abolition of visas, passports, opening of borders, and the abolition of immigration control.

States are still necessary and still serve military purposes.

Wars have only changed their form, but have not disappeared anywhere.

Probably, one can talk about “state as a service”, but with one important caveat: it will be a very expensive service, and only for a very select few. However, such a service already exists in the market, without any Blockchains. Want to live and work in the USA - a EB-5 visa for $1,000,000 is always available. Want to live in Zurich, 300 thousand francs a year, and welcome. And so on. The borders have long been open. But only for the wealthy.

Will they ever be open to everyone? I don’t think so. Will a child born in the slums of Bombay ever be able to reach the age of 18 and decide that it is better to be a citizen of the United States, live in Miami, work as a programmer there, and pay taxes there - press a couple of buttons and get an American passport? No, this will never or almost never happen.

Unfortunately or fortunately, it is hard to say.
