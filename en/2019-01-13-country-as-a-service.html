“Государство как услуга. Почему нет? Потому что.”

Historically, states were necessary, among other things, for more effective conduct of wars. The larger the group, the easier it was for them to defeat smaller aggressors, the easier it was to capture adjacent territories. In order to conduct war more effectively, technologies, science, education, culture, medicine, and much more were needed. However, all of this could be done without establishing states and setting rigid boundaries between them. Countries, flags, anthems, borders, and national interests are needed where there is war. And there is war everywhere and always.

However, the world is changing and technologies are developing. First and foremost, communication technologies and labor automation. Thanks to the internet and robots, physical presence in the place of creating value-added products is becoming less important for an increasing number of professions. Programmers, of course, first and foremost. Twenty years ago, it was very important for them to be near the product they create because the product was located on expensive servers, and telephone or email communication alone was not enough for efficient work. Now the servers have moved to the cloud, and communication has become no less, and often more effective through video conferences or online chats.

Architects, designers, engineers, musicians, bloggers, writers, journalists, artists, managers, marketers, copywriters, producers, investors, analysts, and many other professions that are not tied to the land and do not require manual labor, now often have the freedom to choose their place of residence and even country. Why live where you were born if you can live where it’s warmer? Why pay high income tax in France if you can pay low or zero tax in Dubai? Why rent an apartment in San Francisco for $5,000 when you can write the same articles for a California weekly while renting a bungalow in Bali for $500?

We can broaden the question even further, as Pavel Cherkashin did in his recent article “The homeland-as-a-service model: How blockchain will disrupt the world order”: why not turn the state into a service and its citizens into clients? Everyone will be free to decide in which state they want to live, choosing from all the available options on the market.

Suppose Belgium offers quality healthcare but charges high taxes. In Brazil, taxes are lower, but the crime situation is tense. In the United States, taxes are reasonable and the crime rate is not as high, but public transportation and roads are of low quality. And so on. Each state will have something to offer its clients. Something like office rentals or the currently fashionable coworking spaces - each with its own conditions, price, pros, and cons.

In his article, Pavel Cherkashin predicts that very soon “states will have to compete for the most qualified citizens, offering the best services and advantages such as a strong economy, low taxes, a fair legal system, and reliable social security programs”.

Sounds beautiful! Isn’t this a utopia?

Authors of such ideas about free migration, being enthusiastic, forget why states are needed in the first place. As mentioned above, they are needed for conducting conquering and liberating wars among people. Despite the fact that a small percentage of the population of this planet enjoys studying new technologies and writing reasonable articles about them (including myself), the overwhelming majority struggle for survival in a semi-war or war mode.

According to information from the World Bank, half of the world’s population lives below the poverty line. The dynamics of social inequality, according to a recent report from the World Inequality Lab, are disappointing. The gap between the poor and the rich is increasing: “the share of national income going to the top 10% earners is 37% in Europe, 41% in China, 46% in Russia, 47% in the US and Canada, and about 55% in Sub-Saharan Africa, Brazil and India, and 61% in the Middle East.”

Will all these people, living on a few dollars a month, be offered a choice of country of residence? They have already made their choice. The United States. And now specifically for them, Donald Trump is building a wall. So that they cannot realize their choice.

The United States, like most countries in Western Europe, is in a state of escalating semi-war conflicts with countries where there is less money, but the desire to live well is no less. The flow of immigration from the Middle East to Europe is growing rapidly. This is not yet a third world war, but it is definitely not the time to talk about lifting visas, passports, opening borders, and abolishing immigration control.

States are still needed and still for military purposes.

Wars have only changed their form, but have not disappeared anywhere.

Perhaps we can talk about the “state as a service,” but with one important caveat: it will be a very expensive service, and only for the very privileged. But such a service already exists in the market, without any blockchains. Want to live and work in the US - the EB-5 visa for $1,000,000 is always available. Want to live in Zurich, 300,000 francs a year, and welcome. And so on. Borders have long been open. But only for the rich.

Will they ever be open to everyone? I don’t think so. Will a child ever be able, upon reaching the age of 18 and deciding that it is better to be a citizen of the US, to press a couple of buttons and obtain an American passport, having been born in the slums of Bombay, living in Miami, working as a programmer there, and paying taxes there? No, this will never or almost never happen.

Unfortunately or fortunately, it is difficult to say.
