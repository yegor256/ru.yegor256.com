We don’t need such startupers, they belong in a different section.

In the passport, special attention should be paid to the place of birth. Even if the passport is issued by Ukraine, the place of birth can be, for example, Saint Petersburg. Or, even worse, Leningrad. Such entrepreneurs must be categorically excluded from the Ukrainian section.

Special attention should be paid to the “nationality” field, where it should clearly state “Ukrainian” for males or “Ukrainian” for females. No “Belarusian”, “Tatar”, or, God forbid, “Russian”. This is the Ukrainian section, not any other!

Next, it is necessary to check the mother’s surname. If it is Goltsman or Katsenelenbogen, then it is clear that this startup founder cannot be called Ukrainian. They should be placed in a different section at the exhibition, according to their nationality.

However, if the “nationality” graph matches and the parents’ surnames do not raise suspicions, it is worth taking a close look at the appearance of the startup founder: it can speak for itself. It will be easy to determine and filter out a Kazakh, an Indian, or, let’s say, a black person, no matter how much they try to hide their nationality behind passport data. It will be more difficult with Jews, Russians, or, let’s say, Estonians. It will be harder to distinguish them, but it is also possible, for example, based on external anthropometric characteristics.

And finally, it is necessary to conduct a detailed interview with each startup founder to determine their political views and their attitude towards the annexation of Crimea, Ukraine’s unity, the role of OUN-UPA in World War II, and Vladimir Putin as the main villain of modern times.
