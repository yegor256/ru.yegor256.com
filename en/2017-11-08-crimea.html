But the most important thing is that happy people live there.

I don’t care at all under which flag they live there, which God they pray to, what language they speak, what nationality they are, what their country is called, and who their president is. If they are happy and glad to see me, I will consider Crimea “mine”. I will go there for vacations, just like I go to Italy, Egypt, Mexico, or the Carpathian Mountains.

There is no point in discussing what happened in March 2014: whether the referendum was legitimate, whether one and a half million people really participated in it, whether they were forced or someone among them to put checkmarks where they didn’t want to, whether they were threatened, and whether the ballots were forged. What matters is something else - what do we, the residents of Kiev, Lviv, Odessa, and Kharkiv, want from the residents of Crimea now and what did we want before?

I have described what I want, and it is what I wanted before as well. I want them to be happy and hospitable. If they need to raise Turkish flags over their houses for that, it does not concern me at all. I will only support them in this decision. If they like the flag of Russia, let them raise it. If tomorrow they want to raise the Ukrainian flag again, I will not object. Because it is not my business. It is their choice.

Commonplace and political blindness, you might say? Not at all.
