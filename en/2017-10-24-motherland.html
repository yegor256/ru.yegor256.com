“Моя ли это страна?”

A few days ago, an American acquaintance of mine asked me if I am concerned about my country due to its difficult situation. He meant both the war in Donbass, the current economic state of Ukraine, and everything taken together as it appears from across the ocean. I replied that I do not consider this country as my own, so I don’t have much to worry about. He was surprised. Patriotism seems to be in the blood of Americans. Apparently, I don’t have it. But should I?

Culture. Many years ago, in school, during the times of the Soviet Union, I was taught to be proud of my Motherland and its culture. And there was something to be proud of. In the ranking of the best world writers, poets, composers, artists, or directors, Russia always held leading positions, which were successfully inherited by the Soviet Union. Now I have a different, imposed on me, Motherland, whose culture does not occupy any serious positions in any rankings. Instead of Dostoevsky, Bulgakov, and Pelevin, they “forced” on me third-rate Shevchenko, Karpenko-Karyi, and Zhadan. These are not “my” writers, I don’t want to read them and I cannot be proud of them.

В борьбе с культурой народ на нашей стороне. Аркадий Давидович

Language. The country increasingly speaks Ukrainian, a language foreign to me. Moreover, I am prohibited from using my native Russian language in official communication with government bodies, when concluding contracts at the bank, when watching movies at the cinema, when listening to news on central television channels, and even when consuming advertising. In my own country, I feel like a national minority, whose interests, as Russian propaganda accurately points out, are increasingly being infringed upon. The country in which I was born and raised did not speak this language. This language is being imposed on me. It is not “my” language.

Information. In my country, I am prohibited from using the Internet, at least partially. Just like in Iran, access to Twitter is blocked, and in China, access to Facebook is also blocked. In my country, access to Vk.com, Ok.ru, Auto.ru, and other websites is also blocked. Just like the KGB used to jam “Radio Liberty” for my parents, now they “jam” Vkontakte for me and prohibit undesirable literature. I cannot consider this country mine if it treats my constitutional right to information in such a way. This is not my country.

Politics. Whether it’s the progressive degradation of the population or the skillful actions of the ruling elite in recent years that have raised anti-Russian sentiments to the level of absurdity and mass patriotism to the level of idiocy. As a result, in practical terms, this has resulted in the loss of Crimea and Donbass, and for me personally, the inability to fly directly from Ukraine to Russia. Now a train ticket from Odessa to Moscow costs 7130 hryvnia (~$285), while a flight from Odessa to Minsk to Moscow costs $153. I can’t find any logic in this mathematics, just as I can’t find it in the fact that entering Crimea is now considered a criminal offense. I don’t want to live by these new rules, and I can only tolerate them.

Power. The country’s power is held by blatant criminals, chosen by an openly foolish people. My people regularly gather in squares to forcibly change the power and redistribute property or financial flows. I cannot respect such behavior and do not want to consider that people “my own” who are not really fighting for their freedom. I also have no respect for the power that robs and humiliates its people. This is not my power and not my people.

And I’m not even talking about the fact that the population of my country is on the brink of extinction due to poverty mainly because of corruption. I’m not talking about the fact that my people are one of the leaders in the world in terms of drug consumption. I’m not talking about the fact that there are only a few African countries with a poorer healthcare system than Ukraine. With all this, perhaps we could fight and maybe even win. But only if it was “my” country.

Украину я таковой не считаю, хотя формально и являюсь пока ее гражданином.

К сожалению.
