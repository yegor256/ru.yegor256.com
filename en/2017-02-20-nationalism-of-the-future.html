“Национализм будущего”

People, nation, ethnicity, ethnic group. These and similar terms refer to groups of people who identify themselves based on various common characteristics, such as language, customs, ideology, or religion. In any case, as noted by Lev Gumilyov, “we are such-and-such, and all others are different.”

According to Vakhtang Parshvanov, the formation and existence of different nations is an objective consequence of the process of socialization. In other words, for a person, as a “social being,” it is necessary to feel a sense of belonging to a group, which the person will create, even if it is nonexistent.

Thus, finding in oneself and in one’s surroundings the characteristics that distinguish one from “others” and then elevating these characteristics to the status of national identity is a constructive process that contributes to the individual’s survival.

However, the inevitable consequence of the formation of a people and a nation is nationalism - the “measles of humanity,” as Albert Einstein put it. The meaning of this characterization is that it is a childhood disease that humanity, like a child, must overcome in the early stages of its development, when the key to the success of a tribe, city, or state is the timely physical destruction or subjugation of surrounding tribes, cities, and states. In these conditions, nationalism, or even open chauvinism and xenophobia, unite the population and help it survive.

Nationalism is necessary and very useful in the presence of an external threat. For example, Ivan Ilyin equated nationalism with the “instinct of national self-preservation” and believed that this instinct is “a true and justified state.” It is a kind of immunity of a social group that protects it from destruction or absorption by another larger and more stable group. In simpler terms, it is important for us to instill in ourselves and our children a hatred of strangers if we want our own children to survive.

There are other opinions, for example, Andrei Konchalovsky believes that “nationalism in its constructive forms is, above all, serving one’s own people, rather than destroying other peoples in the name of one’s own.” This is a rose-colored view. As shown above, the very concept of dividing the population into nations and ethnic groups was born solely for military purposes: to identify one’s own and develop a sense of dislike for others. Wherever the rhetoric of “us versus them” is used, it is about war. When the second part of this pair is removed and only a call to “serve one’s own people” is made, as Konchalovsky did, it smells of nothing but hypocrisy.

Now let’s focus on the interesting situation with the assessment of the total number of peoples and nations on the planet. According to various opinions, there are currently between 800 and 2000 of them in the world. Wikipedia lists only a few hundred. In other sources, I found even more ambiguous figures: from several hundred to several thousand. Why is there no exact list of them, considering that we are talking about groups consisting of tens of thousands of people or more? Where does this inaccuracy come from?

It’s simple, mainly due to technological progress, the borders between nations are becoming blurred and disappearing: we no longer know who is who.

The defining trends on the ethnic map of our planet at the moment are consolidation and assimilation. In other words, it is becoming increasingly difficult to say who among us is Russian, Ukrainian, Jewish, Tatar, or Black. Surprisingly, many scientists even consider the term “race” in relation to humans to have lost its scientific relevance. In other words, it is becoming more difficult to objectively determine the nationality of a specific inhabitant of our planet, and there is less real need for it.

We are born in the USSR to a Ukrainian mother and a Belarusian father; we live in Berlin; we speak English in the office, Russian with our wife, German with our children, and Turkish in the supermarket; we believe in Jesus Christ, do not celebrate Easter, but celebrate Soviet Army Day; we wear Chinese jeans; we love Italian cuisine and French comedies. What nationality are we? What nationality will our children be, who will speak Russian with a strong German accent only when asked to? None?

It is absolutely obvious that such dividing characteristics as place of birth, language, cultural traditions, religious beliefs, as well as skull shape and eye shape, are losing their relevance. Moreover, the use of these outdated characteristics, according to Parcavaniya, can only indicate the “narrow-mindedness and backwardness” of the people who use them.

However, the desire to gather in groups and identify oneself through belonging to them has not disappeared. It is innate and vital, as we have seen above. In other words, nationalism, chauvinism, and xenophobia are uneradicable because they are natural. The question is only how those who are not “backward and limited” will identify the “other”.

Nationalism of the future, if civilization continues to develop and not degrade, will divide people into groups and communities based on such characteristics as professional interests (for example, artists, programmers, writers, fashion designers), life convictions (for example, vegetarians, childfree, downshifters), sports (for example, bodybuilders, snowboarders, cyclists), music, and others. These groups and communities are the “nations of the future”.

These new nations are already forming, if you observe the development of communities, for example, on Facebook. Alongside politicized groups dedicated to hatred towards “vatniks” or “Ukrops”, groups of vegetarians or movie enthusiasts are equally, and often much more popular.

The more developed a person is, the more educated and oriented towards self-development, the less significant the outdated categories of national division are for them. At the same time, their desire to identify themselves through belonging to a group and contrasting themselves with the “others” does not disappear. It is realized through the formation of new nations and peoples that have nothing in common with Ukrainians, Russians, Chinese, or Jews. Now they are called “coffee lovers,” “theater actors,” “travel bloggers,” and “followers of rebirthing”.

Sooner or later, the Ukrainian nation will cease to exist, just like the Georgian, Tatar, Spanish, or Russian nation. The Ukrainian language, literature, music, cuisine, rituals, and traditions will remain, but there will be no “Ukrainian people.” This concept will simply become outdated.
