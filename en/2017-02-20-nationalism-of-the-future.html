“Национализм будущего”

Nation, people, ethnicity, ethnos. These and similar terms refer to groups of people identifying themselves based on various common characteristics, such as language, customs, ideology, or religion. In any case, as Lev Gumilev noted, the unchanging unifying message for them is: “we are such-and-such, and all others are different”.

According to Vakhushti Parcvenia, education and the existence of different nations are an objective consequence of the process of socialization. In other words, for a person to survive, as a “social being”, it is necessary to feel a sense of belonging to a group, which a person will create even in its absence, even in an empty space.

Thus, finding signs of distinction within oneself and one’s surroundings and then elevating these signs to the level of national identity is a constructive process that contributes to individual survival.

However, a natural consequence of the formation of a people and a nation is nationalism - the “measles of humanity,” in the words of Albert Einstein. The meaning of this characteristic is that it is a childhood disease that humanity, like a child, must overcome in the early stages of its development, when the key to the success of a tribe, city, or state is the timely physical destruction or enslavement of surrounding tribes, cities, and states. In these conditions, nationalism, or even open chauvinism and xenophobia, will unite the population and help it survive.

Nationalism is necessary and very useful in the presence of an external threat. For example, Ivan Ilyin identified nationalism with the “instinct of national self-preservation” and believed that this instinct is “a true and justified state.” This is a kind of immunity of a social group, protecting it from destruction or absorption by another larger and more stable group. In simpler terms, it is important for us to cultivate in ourselves and our children a hatred for foreigners if we want our own children to survive.

There are different opinions, for example, Andrey Konchalovsky believes that “nationalism in its constructive forms is, first and foremost, serving one’s own people, not the destruction of other peoples in the name of one’s own.” This is a rose-colored view. As shown above, the concept of dividing the population into nations and nationalities was born solely for military purposes: to identify one’s own and develop a sense of dislike towards others. Everywhere rhetoric of “us versus them” is used, it is about war. When the second part of this pair is removed and only a call to “serve one’s own people” is made, as Konchalovsky did, it smells of nothing but hypocrisy.

Now let’s pay attention to an interesting situation regarding the estimation of the total number of peoples and nations on the planet. According to various opinions, currently there are from 800 to 2000 of them in the world. Wikipedia lists only a few hundred. Other sources provide even more indefinite figures: from several hundreds to several thousands. Why is there no exact list of them, considering that we are talking about groups consisting of tens of thousands of people or more? Where does such inaccuracy come from?

It’s simple, mainly thanks to technological progress, the boundaries between nations blur and disappear: we no longer know who is who.

The defining trends on the ethnic map of our planet at the moment are consolidation and assimilation. In other words, it is becoming increasingly difficult to determine who among us is Russian, Ukrainian, Jewish, Tatar or African. Surprisingly, many scientists even consider the term “race” in relation to humans to have lost its scientific relevance. In other words, it is becoming more and more difficult to objectively determine the nationality of a specific inhabitant of our planet, and there is less and less real necessity in this.

We are born in the USSR to a Ukrainian mother and a Belarusian father; we live in Berlin; we speak English at the office, Russian with our wife, German with our children, and Turkish at the supermarket; we believe in Jesus Christ, we do not celebrate Easter, but we celebrate Soviet Army Day; we wear Chinese jeans; we love Italian cuisine and French comedies. What nationality are we? What nationality will our children be, who will speak Russian with a strong German accent only when asked to do so? None?

It is absolutely obvious that such signs of division by nations as place of birth, language, cultural traditions, religious beliefs, as well as cranial shape and eye slit, lose their relevance. Moreover, the use of these outdated signs, according to Parcva’s words, can only indicate the “narrow-mindedness and backwardness” of the people who use them.

However, the desire to gather in groups and identify oneself through belonging to them has not disappeared. As we have seen above, it is innate and vital. In other words, nationalism, chauvinism, and xenophobia are ineradicable because they are natural. The question is only how those who are not “backward and limited” will identify the “other”.

The nationalism of the future, if civilization continues to develop rather than degrade, will divide people into groups and communities based on such characteristics as professional interests (for example, artists, programmers, writers, fashion designers), life beliefs (for example, vegetarians, childfree, downshifters), sports (for example, bodybuilders, snowboarders, cyclists), music, and others. These groups and communities are precisely the “nations of the future”.

These new nations are already forming if you observe the development of communities, for example on Facebook. Alongside politicized groups dedicated to hatred towards “vatniks” or “ukrams”, groups of vegetarians or lovers of good cinema are no less, and often significantly more popular.

The more developed a person is, the more educated and oriented towards self-development, the less significant outdated categories of national division are for him. At the same time, his desire to identify himself through belonging to a group and contrasting himself with “others” does not go away. It is realized through the formation of new nations and peoples that have nothing in common with Ukrainians, Russians, Chinese, or Jews. Now they are called “coffee lovers,” “theater actors,” “tourist bloggers,” and “followers of rebirthing.”

Sooner or later, the Ukrainian nation will cease to exist, just like the Georgian, Tatar, Spanish, or Russian nations. The Ukrainian language, literature, music, cuisine, rituals, and traditions will remain, but there will be no “Ukrainian people.” This concept will simply outlive itself.
