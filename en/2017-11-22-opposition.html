“В оппозиции к народу”

Recently, an acquaintance asked me if I am opposed to the government. The question made me think, and soon I found an unexpected answer that surprised both my interlocutor and myself. I am not opposed to the government - I am opposed to my own people. I see not the government, but our own Ukrainian people as the main enemy. I am in uncompromising opposition to them. I consider them responsible for all the troubles and I believe it is necessary to urgently purify them.

In my strong belief, the main political conflict in Ukraine is not the power-opposition conflict, but the conflict between the smart people and the fools. It is evident that the number of the latter is rapidly increasing.

Both in power and in opposition, there have been non-poor and non-stupid people for many years, successfully exploiting the foolish and obedient population. The problem is not with them. They are not the root of evil, they are only its consequence.

Evil is gullibility, greed, political illiteracy, ignorance, thievery, sycophancy, and the irresponsibility of citizens. Such a people rightfully have the power and opposition that they have. Ukrainian society needs not parliamentary opposition in power, but a nationwide opposition to fools.

Here are four categories of “fools” that, in my opinion, are the most dangerous and need to be fought against as a priority, at all levels and whenever possible:

Grechkoedy. This is what they call those who vote in elections, figuratively speaking, for a package of buckwheat. At first glance, these people can be understood - they are far from politics, rightfully do not believe that one candidate-criminal is better than another candidate-policeman, they have long been disappointed in any authority, and are taking money. Their behavior, which is justified in the moment but strategically destructive, turns the whole nation into an easily manageable herd, which various Korbans and Filatovs successfully exploit. By selling their voting rights, “grechkoedy” turn democracy into buckwheatocracy, and the power of the people into the power of stolen money from this people. How to defeat the grechkoedy? It is not the oligarchs in parliament, who are unreachable, but the people living next to us, who enslave and impoverish us with their apathy, greed, and stupidity. To fight them, there is no need to set fire to tires and destroy tents on Maidan. It is enough to simply punch them in the face explain the seriousness and danger of their behavior.

Patriots. Neither common sense, nor expediency, nor logic, nor sober reasoning, nor plans, nor arguments of reason are capable of extinguishing the flames of righteous patriotic xenophobia. Patriots seek enemies, not reasons. Patriots dream of victory “over” rather than “for”. Patriots need a reason to hate, not reasons to love. As Oscar Wilde said, “patriotism is a virtue of the vicious”. Patriots cover vices with bright flags, emblems, anthems, and illustrated books about the rich history of “their” people. Patriotism does not know how to think, it knows how to act. To act under the guidance of those who think. It is necessary to fight against patriotism at all levels, from the everyday and beyond. Although, at higher levels it almost does not exist. It thrives at the bottom, where long reflections are not in honor, but where flags are brighter.

Activists. At the beginning of the last century, in the Soviet labor camps of the GULAG system, there was a category of prisoners who collaborated with the administration. They were called “activists”. They helped the administration maintain order and discipline among the prisoners. Ukraine, in its current state, with its impoverished people living practically in slavery, differs little from a Stalinist concentration camp. Various activists, who call on us to clean up courtyards and roadsides, raise funds to help children, ecologists, and fighters for European values, play into the hands of the corrupt ruling class, distracting the population from the real problem. It is not sick children who need to be saved, but officials from the healthcare system who need to be imprisoned. It is not about raising funds for the fight against leukemia, but about investigating the multimillion-dollar embezzlement from the country’s budget. You cannot cure a cancerous tumor with Zelenka (a green antiseptic liquid), but it is precisely this kind of treatment that “activists” of all types propose. I urge you to carefully consider the fighters for “good at any cost” — they are traitors.

Bribe-lovers. “He doesn’t take bribes, people thank him on their own” - such a characterization of an “honest” official can be heard very often, in a very positive context. Bribery is such a commonplace and familiar phenomenon for us that many simply do not realize that it is a crime. We have committees to combat corruption, slogans and high-profile criminal cases, but we still consider a judge in a Mercedes and the head of the regional administration in a Lexus as “respected and decent” people, because they are not bribe-takers and not corrupt officials, people are just “grateful” to them. Our tolerant attitude towards bribes at the everyday level is the foundation of total corruption in Ukraine. Let’s change our attitude towards expensive suits and watches of people in power - they are thieves, not “respected” people.

Recently, I was asked what exactly we, specifically you and I, can do to change the situation in our impoverished and devastated Ukraine? I think that we need to start by fighting against these four categories of our enemies. Fighting at our available level, in everyday communication, in social networks, in offices and at home.
