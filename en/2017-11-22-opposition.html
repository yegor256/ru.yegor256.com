“В оппозиции к народу”

Recently, a acquaintance asked me if I am opposed to the government. The question made me think, and soon the answer I found surprised my interlocutor and myself a little. I am not opposed to the government - I am opposed to my own people. I see not the authorities as the main enemy, but our own Ukrainian people. I am in uncompromising opposition to them. I consider them to be the cause of all misfortunes and I consider it necessary to urgently purge them.

In my deep conviction, the main political conflict in Ukraine is not the conflict between the authorities and the opposition, but the conflict between the intelligent and the fools. It is evident that the number of the latter is rapidly growing.

Both in power and in opposition, there are wealthy and intelligent people who have been successfully exploiting the foolish and obedient people for many years. The problem is not with them. They are not the root of evil, they are only its consequence.

Evil is gullibility, greed, political illiteracy, ignorance, thievery, servility, and irresponsibility of citizens. Such a people rightfully has the power and opposition that it has. Ukrainian society needs not parliamentary opposition to the government, but a nationwide opposition to fools.

Here are four categories of “fools” which, in my opinion, are the most dangerous and with which we must fight in the first place, at all levels and at any opportunity:

Grechko-eaters. This is what those are called who vote in elections, figuratively speaking, for a package of buckwheat. At first glance, these people can be understood. They are far from politics, they rightfully do not believe that one candidate-bandit is better than another candidate-cop, they have long been disappointed in any authority, and they take money for their votes. This behavior, which is momentarily justified but strategically devastating, turns the whole people into easily manageable herd, which various Korban and Filatov types successfully use. By selling their right to vote, “grechko-eaters” turn democracy into buckwheatocracy and the power of the people into the power of this people’s stolen money. How to defeat grechko-eaters? It is not the oligarchs in parliament, who are unreachable, but the people who live next to us and drive us into slavery and poverty with their apathy, greed, and stupidity. To fight them, there is no need to set fire to tires and tear down tents on Maidan. It is enough to simply explain the seriousness and danger of their behavior.

Patriots. Common sense, expediency, logic, sober reasoning, plans, and rational arguments are incapable of extinguishing the flames of righteous patriotic xenophobia. Patriots seek enemies, not causes. Patriots dream of victory “over” rather than victory “for”. Patriots need a reason to hate, not reasons to love. As Oscar Wilde said, “patriotism is the virtue of the vicious”. Patriots hide vices behind bright banners, emblems, anthems, and illustrated books about the rich history of “their” people. Patriotism does not know how to think, it knows how to act. To act under the guidance of those who think. It is necessary to fight patriotism at all levels, from everyday life and beyond. Although, there is hardly any at higher levels. It thrives at the bottom, where long reflections are not appreciated, and banners shine brighter.

Activists. At the beginning of the last century, in the Soviet labor camps of the GULAG system, there was a category of prisoners who collaborated with the administration. They were called “activists”. They helped the administration maintain order and discipline among the prisoners. Ukraine in its present state, with its impoverished population living practically in slavery, is not much different from Stalin’s concentration camps. Various activists who call for cleanliness in yards and on roadsides, raise funds for children’s aid, ecological activists, and fighters for European values pour water on the mills of the ruling class of corrupt officials, distracting the population from the real problem. We need to imprison officials of the healthcare system, not save sick children. We need to investigate the multimillion embezzlement from the country’s budget, not collect funds for fighting leukemia. You cannot cure a cancerous tumor with aloe vera, but this is exactly the treatment that “activists” of all kinds offer. I urge you to take a closer look at the fighters for “good at any cost” - they are traitors.

Bribe-lovers. “He does not take bribes, he is just thanked by others” - such a characteristic of an “honest” official is often heard in a very positive context. Bribery is such a common and familiar phenomenon for us that many simply do not realize that it is a crime. We have committees to fight corruption, slogans, and high-profile criminal cases, but we still consider a judge in a Mercedes and the head of the regional administration in a Lexus as “respected and decent” people because they are not bribe-takers or corrupt officials, they are just “grateful” people. Our tolerant attitude towards bribes at the everyday level is the foundation of total corruption in Ukraine. Let’s change our attitude towards expensive suits and watches of people in power - they are thieves, not “respected” people.

Recently, I was asked what exactly we, specifically you and I, can do to change the situation in our impoverished and ruined Ukraine? I think we should start by fighting these four categories of our enemies. Fight at the level accessible to us, in everyday communication, on social networks, in offices, and at home.

Our nationwide troubles begin with these people.
