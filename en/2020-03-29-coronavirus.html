They convinced me that Ded Moroz was real, and the janitor, who for some reason was not at the performance, simply fell ill that day.

Many years passed, and I learned that my childhood desire to find an alternative explanation for what was happening, contrary to the accepted version, is called a conspiracy theory. Like adult conspiracy theorists, I didn’t have indisputable evidence at that time, and I couldn’t uncover the conspiracy. Ded Moroz (Russian counterpart of Santa Claus) was exposed later, when I became a full member of the closed group “adults,” where children are not allowed for their own good. In the end, I learned the main lesson - being a conspiracy theorist is dangerous and unprofitable. No one will believe you anyway, and you will waste a lot of nerves and energy.

Later, much more serious stories replaced the harmless Grandfather, in which it was even harder to believe than in a fairytale character, despite the insistence of all official sources. Do you doubt that everything was exactly like that? You are a conspiracy theorist and a supporter of the moon landing hoax.

Or take, for example, the unfinished story of how the planet was struck by a previously unknown to science coronavirus, spreading at the speed of medieval plague and threatening all of us with inevitable and agonizing death… from a runny nose.
