If we believe historians, first there were empires, emperors, and slavery. Then the empires fell because they became ashamed, and the dark years of medieval feudalism came, which at some point bored all enlightened people, and under the cries of the newly discovered Native Americans, the New Time came, and then the Renaissance. Slavery was abolished not immediately, but democracy was also not implemented immediately. All former slaves were given equal rights by printing laws and constitutions for them. Kings and emperors were either hanged or renamed as “constitutional monarchies”, thus freeing the individual from the oppression of other individuals, but in vain…

The main achievement of many years of our struggle for freedom is the recognition of the right of every individual to have their own beliefs. What prevented them from having them before the victory of liberalism? Not what, but who. Other individuals hindered the freedom-loving individual, who unsurprisingly also had their own beliefs. From the king to the provincial landowner, everyone had their own opinion, which had to be taken into account by the serf. And not just taken into account, but obeyed someone else’s will, sometimes under the threat of death. It’s good that all of this is already in the distant past.

Liberal reforms depersonalized power, replacing the owner with parliament, and his good or evil will with laws. In dark times, runaway peasants were returned to the feudal lord, who both executed and showed mercy. In today’s victorious democracy, border violators are caught by the customs control service, and the penitentiary system punishes them. Interestingly, we are satisfied with the power of laws, while the power of a specific person causes rejection. We are much more willing to be slaves to the constitution, the police, and society than to an individual. We replaced the power of specific authorities with the authority of nameless power.

The USA, as the guardian of liberalism, has been attacking its opponents with the same weapon for many decades: “the lack of power alternation speaks of limitations on freedom!” It doesn’t matter how the dictator behaves - their prolonged presence in power is a threat to the liberal world order. No matter what freedoms they bestow upon their subjects, no matter how successful the economy is, no matter how just their will may be, and no matter what support they enjoy from their people - they are usurpers and medieval tyrants. The freedom-loving people must overthrow them and establish the rule of impersonal laws and corrupt parliaments.

The civilized world takes pride in the rotation of power. Presidential and parliamentary elections, capturing the attention of the entire population of the country, regularly remind us that the person in power will be replaced in the near future. Why will they be replaced? Because they failed? Turned out to be a deceiver? Did not live up to the trust? No! They will be replaced because we do not recognize anyone in power, except for the power of laws. We do not acknowledge authorities in power. We do not recognize the power and authority of another person over us. Each of us is a unique and inviolable individual, with our own inner world, beliefs, opinions, and understanding of life. For some time, we are willing to tolerate another chatterbox in a suit behind the lectern, but to admit that they are the executors of our destinies - never!

To further strengthen our belief in our victory over the forces of despotism, they show us the insignificance and vulnerability of people in power. We are no longer surprised by the corruption and sexual scandals surrounding US presidents, it’s time to put one of them behind bars. It’s so democratic that presidents know how to fall in love, get divorced, and accept bribes. Not like the kings of the time of Louis, about whom the commoners only knew that they were chosen by God and would execute their subjects in the most brutal ways at any convenient opportunity.

We, ordinary people, have won! The power belongs to us! The kings have been overthrown! Only clowns are left (sometimes, quite literally, we remember Reagan and Zelensky), who have little influence! Everything depends on us, on our will, on our vote!

Neither power nor resources can be depersonalized, they cannot belong to everyone at once. A person is not only a social being, but also a herd animal. It is inherent in us to desire to form hierarchies and submit to leaders. The apparent absence of dictators and power pyramids with individuals subordinated to them only indicates that their existence is carefully hidden. Both power and resources have long been distributed among those who realized in time that the profession of a king has become unsafe and went into corporate shadow, leaving well-paid puppets on the podiums.

Can we, the people, control them if we don’t even know the names of these plutocrats? We are familiar with the names of “bloody” dictators. Returning to the reasons: our liberal rejection of “royal power” led us to the rule of oligarchy. We are bribed with promises of “freedom and equality,” but in the end, we are sold democracy in its most repugnant forms: with fake politicians and the absolute irresponsibility of the ruling elite.
