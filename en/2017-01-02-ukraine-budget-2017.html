Last week, the Verkhovna Rada adopted the budget for 2017 proposed by the Cabinet of Ministers. A few days later, on NewsOne, Mustafa Nayem analyzed this event and praised the Cabinet for its timeliness, but called the final stage a farce, because, in his opinion, the opposition forces received bribes in exchange for supporting the government. In turn, I consider Nayem’s speech a farce.

I have a metaphor in mind. Imagine that we are preparing a gymnast for competitions. Everything would be fine, but he often falls off the bars. Among other problems, he has a brain tumor.

We are aware of this disease, but he also has other difficulties. Firstly, we suspect that he has a tight shirt that restricts his movements: we are trying to find a better quality uniform. Secondly, it seems that he has weak forearm muscles: apparently, we should include more exercises for this muscle group in the training program. Thirdly, apparently, he is overweight: losing a few kilograms would not hurt.

So, we have four problems: tight uniform, weak muscles, excess weight, and a brain tumor.

Of course, we understand that most likely he regularly falls on the mats because he loses consciousness, and he needs not the bars, but intensive chemotherapy with a very low chance of survival at all. We know this, but there are also other problems, such as a tight uniform and excess weight. Shouldn’t we address them too?

It sounds absurd, doesn’t it? What do excess weight and a tight uniform have to do with it if the athlete’s life itself is at risk? Without solving the main problem, there is no point in even mentioning the rest.

The same thing is happening in Ukraine right now. The entire state apparatus of this country is deeply affected by corruption. Ukraine ranks 130th in a well-known rating, worse than us are only twenty-something countries, including Congo, Uganda, Syria, Iraq, and Somalia. Corruption—is a malignant tumor that prevents our country from getting back on its feet.

What does the budget have to do with it? What difference does it make whether it was adopted on time or with a delay? What will change if it is of good quality or if there are mistakes in it? What role does this document play in a country where not a single power institution functions?

Mustafa, are you suggesting that we fight the athlete’s excess weight when he has a brain tumor? Either you don’t understand the situation, or you are paid just like the opposition members you accuse.
