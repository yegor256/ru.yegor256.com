Racism, anti-Semitism, nationalism, chauvinism, sexism, homophobia, and other forms of intra-species xenophobia are considered by Konrad Lorenz, the author of the book “The So-Called Evil,” to be the most serious dangers threatening humanity in modern cultural, historical, and technological conditions. Lorenz believes that they are the result of intra-species selection that has been operating on our ancestors for tens of thousands of years, while Erich Fromm, in his book “The Anatomy of Human Destructiveness,” sees them as one of the main obstacles to human progress. What can be done and how can this evil be eradicated?

Let’s assume that the scientists are right and there is an urgent need to do something about it so that it does not hinder progress and threaten humanity (although it has been standing for tens of thousands of years and progress has somehow managed with it; however, let’s leave this logical inconsistency on the conscience of the scientists). Do campaigns against, for example, racism, help to defeat it, or do they have the opposite effect?

In 2020, the UN held the “World Conference against Racism, Racial Discrimination, Xenophobia and Related Intolerance” in South Africa, during which it was stated, among other things, that (I quote) “any doctrine of racial superiority is scientifically invalid, morally condemnable, socially unjust, and dangerous, and must be rejected along with theories attempting to establish the existence of separate human races.” How is it possible that there are no doctrines and theories yet, but we a priori deem them scientifically invalid?

Jens Rydgren, a professor of sociology at Stockholm University, writes in his work “The Logic of Xenophobia”: “Racism and xenophobia can appear quite rational from the aggressor’s point of view when they are unaware of or misled about the target of their attack” (my paraphrased translation from English). We don’t have to look far for an example: Borat Sagdiyev, from the eponymous movie, believed that Jews could transform into cockroaches. He was not to blame for having such knowledge in his curly head, and he had no intention of questioning this truth. Based on the amount of information he had about Jews, Borat reasonably harbored dislike towards them.

It is very difficult to make Borat stop hating Jews, whom he believes can turn into insects. The only way to deal with such radical anti-Semitism is to explain or scientifically prove that this version is a fabrication.

It is worth noting that not every xenophobia is as comical as Borat’s fear of Jews. In the overwhelming majority of cases, each hatred has a perfectly logical explanation that underlies the offensive generalization.

For example, according to statistics, more than half of the murders in the USA are committed by African American citizens, while they make up only 13% of the total population. Should a police officer, when apprehending an African American criminal, remember this statistic and take stricter security measures than if he were apprehending, say, an Indian? Would the use of this statistic not be an insult to the specific offender who happened to be African American against his will?

Another example: currently, the overwhelming majority of civil aviation pilots are men. Should an airline conduct additional training and checks, such as stress resistance, when hiring women pilots, since women are less capable of handling it? Should airlines inform passengers that their flight will be piloted by a woman? Wouldn’t such information be an insult to a specific female pilot who may very well be more resilient to stress than the average representative of the social group “women”?

And finally, another example: you are walking with your child and find yourself next to a stadium where a football match has just ended in defeat. You are surrounded by excited people with masks on their faces and cold weapons in their hands. They have not yet started smashing windows and flipping cars, but it is highly likely that it will escalate to that. Will you explain to your child that these are aggressive football fans and there is nothing good to expect from their company - it is better to stay away? It is quite possible that many of them are wonderful people. Moreover, not all football fans smash windows. Your generalization will undoubtedly offend many of them, but it will save your child’s life. What will you choose, lengthy discussions on political correctness and tolerance, or immediate stereotyping and evacuation to a safe place?

I mean that not every xenophobia and even hatred is unfounded and is a prejudice. We need to be able to distinguish one from the other and fight specifically against the latter, getting rid of senseless violence and aggression, which, as we know from Fromm, is the main obstacle to human progress. The only remedy against prejudices can be one thing: enlightenment.

However, the opposite is happening. The UN, as we can see, condemns any theories about the existence of races and instead of identifying contradictions, emphasizing differences, analyzing the strengths and weaknesses of different social groups, all people are recognized as equal. Instead of enlightenment, which is always based on doubts and internal conflicts, we are offered empty slogans like “black lives matter” that are emotionally charged but lack substance. Education is being blocked. Sensitive social topics are tabooed. Scientists are being stripped of honorary titles. Any discussion of inequality that does not contain radical polarizations and unequivocal condemnation of everything negative is recognized as racism and leads to the boycott of anyone who inadvertently enters into such a discussion.

Supposedly, by moving towards a noble goal - the reduction of xenophobia - we achieve the opposite effect because we act with repressive methods. By tabooing the topic and intimidating scientists, we cannot eliminate the outbreak of social, racial, gender, or national conflict. Only through education and enlightenment, which require open discussions and debates primarily. However, discussions are becoming fewer, disputes are prohibited, and dissenters are silenced.
