There have been, are, and will be numerous wars and military conflicts on the planet. However, according to Massimo Morelli, a professor of political science and economics at Columbia University, the majority of conflicts arise from issues of non-compliance with obligations.

If we are talking about wars for independence, then this unfulfilled obligation seems to be an unwritten but obvious commitment not to "bring one's own regulations into someone else's backyard". Thus, the uprisings of Roman provinces hundreds of years ago in Gaul, Macedonia, or Italy were little different in nature from the struggle of Native Americans against the Americans in the 18th century or the Chechens against the Russian Federation in the 20th. In all cases, the owners of the "backyard" were dissatisfied with the foreign "regulations".

Apparently, this is human nature: we do not like being controlled from the outside, even if it is done well. We are willing to tolerate it as long as there is a clear benefit (such as states in the USA), or as long as we are kept by force (such as republics in the USSR).
