Externally, all wars are the same: flags, slogans, heroes, cripples, and victorious marches. In all wars, people die, and calls for unity for the sake of victory over the enemy, for the defense of the homeland, are heard. Each time, soldiers on the opposite side of the field, city, or trench are called the enemy, and the homeland is the place where the coordination of fire is carried out. But are these soldiers really our true enemies, and is the homeland exactly there?

Imagine that you are the owner of a company that manufactures equipment for metallurgical plants, and I am the owner of one of those plants. You live and work in Kharkiv, and my plant is located in Donetsk. You provide me with equipment, and I purchase it from you. With the help of your electronics, I produce and sell metal sheets and reinforcement bars. So, why would I want to fight against you?

Moreover, why would I need to defend myself from you? What are our motives to arm ourselves and blow each other up? What economic sense does that make? The work is going on, enterprises are functioning, people are getting paid, production capacities are growing, profits are increasing, new technologies are being implemented. Why do we need to fight?

Any war, except for economic and competitive ones, is unnecessary for us, manufacturers of goods and services. Moreover, it only harms us.

Кому же она нужна?

The thing is, the proposed picture is missing another important element - a state. We will not be allowed to work and trade peacefully. States will form around us. They will convince us that we are potential enemies to each other, that all you think about is how to rob me, while I dream of seeing your factory in ruins. They will call me Ukrainian and you Russian. I will have a yellow-blue flag, and you a tricolor one. I will have my own national history, and you will have yours. And in order to deliver my goods to you, I will have to go through border control and pay customs fees. We will be divided and fenced off.

Those who do this, namely our governments, will need a war.

War is necessary for the preservation of the state. In most cases, its own people are its main enemy (there are exceptions, but they are rare). In order for us to continue working for the benefit of the officials in the state apparatus, we must constantly be convinced of the value of this apparatus. Otherwise, sooner or later, you and I will sit in two bulldozers and demolish this fence that has been built between us, along with the customs and the checkpoint.

To prevent this from happening, to avoid riots and revolutions, it is necessary to uphold and strengthen the value of the concept of “Homeland”. We all must understand that this land is “ours”, but beyond that fence, a kilometer away, it is already “not ours”. And since the Homeland is so valuable, it must be defended. An army is needed, a war is needed, heroes are needed, sacrifices are needed, monuments are needed, memorials are needed, military-patriotic events are needed. And, of course, payment is required for the defense of the Homeland.

War is necessary for officials but not for us. If we understand this, we will stop celebrating heroes, stop laying flowers at their memorials, and stop hanging national flags on the facades of houses and windshields. We will understand that the flag, anthem, patriotism, and national consciousness are tools of manipulation invented by states against their own people.

The more visible these tools are and the more a state speaks about patriotism, the more strained its relationship with its own people becomes. Consequently, it deserves less trust.
