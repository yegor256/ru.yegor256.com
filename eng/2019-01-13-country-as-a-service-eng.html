Historically, states were needed, among other things, for more effective warfare. The larger the group, the easier it was for them to defeat smaller aggressors and conquer adjacent territories. In order to conduct warfare more efficiently, technologies, science, education, culture, medicine, and much more were needed. However, all of this could be done without the establishment of nation-states and the imposition of rigid borders between them. Countries, flags, anthems, borders, and national interests are necessary where there is war. And there is war everywhere and always.

However, the world is changing and technologies are advancing. First and foremost, communication and labor automation technologies. Thanks to the internet and robots, physical presence in the place of value creation is becoming less important for an increasing number of professions. Programmers, of course, are primarily affected. Just twenty years ago, it was crucial for them to be close to the product they were creating because the product was located on expensive servers, and mere phone or email communication was not enough for effective work. Now, servers have moved to the “cloud,” and communication has become no less, and often even more effective through video conferences or online chats.

Architects, designers, engineers, musicians, bloggers, writers, journalists, artists, managers, marketers, copywriters, producers, investors, analysts, and many other professions that are not tied to the land and do not require manual labor, now often have the freedom to choose their place of residence and even country. Why live where you were born if you can live where it’s warmer? Why pay high income tax in France if you can pay low or zero tax in Dubai? Why rent an apartment in San Francisco for $5,000 when you can write the same articles for the California Weekly while renting a bungalow in Bali for $500?

One can broaden the question further, as Pavel Cherkashin did in his recent article “The homeland-as-a-service model: How blockchain will disrupt the world order” (Russian version here): why not turn a nation into a service and its citizens into customers? Each individual would be free to decide in which country they want to live, choosing from all available options on the market.

Suppose Belgium offers quality healthcare but has high taxes. In Brazil, taxes are lower but the crime situation is tense. In the US, taxes are acceptable and the crime rate is not as high, but public transportation and roads are of low quality. And so on. Each country will have something to offer its clients. It’s similar to renting offices or the currently fashionable co-working spaces - each has its own conditions, price, pros, and cons.

Pavel Cherkashin predicts in his article that very soon “states will have to compete for the most qualified citizens, providing the best services and advantages such as a strong economy, low taxes, a fair legal system, and reliable social security programs.”

Sounds beautiful! Isn’t it a utopia?

Да, она.

The authors of such ideas about free migration, getting carried away, forget about the primary purpose of states. And they are needed, as mentioned above, for conducting aggressive and liberating wars among people. Despite the fact that a small percentage of the population of this planet enjoys exploring new technologies and writing sensible articles about them (myself included), the overwhelming majority struggle for survival in a semi-war or war mode.

According to the World Bank, half of the world’s population lives below the poverty line. The recent report from the World Inequality Lab indicates an alarming trend in social inequality. The gap between the poor and the rich is widening: “the share of national income held by the top 10% earners is 37% in Europe, 41% in China, 46% in Russia, 47% in the United States and Canada, and around 55% in Sub-Saharan Africa, Brazil, and India, and 61% in the Middle East.”

Will all these people living on a few dollars a month be offered a choice of country to live in? They have already chosen. The USA. And now specifically for them, Donald Trump is building a wall. So that they cannot fulfill their choice.

The United States, like most Western European countries, is in a state of escalating semi-military conflict with countries where there is less money but no shortage of desire for a better life. The flow of emigration from the Middle East to Europe is rapidly increasing. This is not yet a third world war, but it is also not a time when one can talk about visa cancellations, passport waivers, open borders, and the abolition of immigration control.

States are still necessary and still serve military purposes.

Wars have only changed their form but have not disappeared.

Perhaps we can talk about “the state as a service,” but with one important caveat: it would be a very expensive service, only available to a select few. However, such a service already exists in the market, without any blockchains. Want to live and work in the United States? The EB-5 visa is always available for $1,000,000. Want to live in Zurich? 300,000 francs a year, and you’re welcome. And so on. The borders have long been open. But only for the wealthy.

Will they ever be open to everyone? I don’t think so. Will a child born in the slums of Bombay ever be able to turn 18, decide that it’s better to be a citizen of the USA, live in Miami, work as a programmer there, and pay taxes there — press a few buttons and get an American passport? No, this will never or almost never happen.

Unfortunately or fortunately, it is hard to say.
