Certainly, such a perspective on the situation is understandable if looked at superficially. Yes, indeed, thieves are to blame for theft, murderers for killings, and corrupt individuals for corruption. It would seem logical to assume that by removing them from power, imprisoning them, or eliminating them, we would eradicate theft, violence, and corruption from society.

But let’s analyze deeper. First of all, let’s agree that all these criminals in power, from Yanukovych to the last tax inspector and patrol policeman, are also human beings. Many of them are our acquaintances. After all, each of you has friends who are corrupt, don’t you?

I am sure they do not have horns or hooves. They are not “bad” people. Furthermore, they are not stupid or lazy. They are the most fortunate and energetic, capable of finding ways to enrich themselves in difficult situations. They are successful people, and that is the most important thing. There is no need to hate or despise them.

FAILED TO TRANSLATE THIS PARAGRAPH

By changing the government and hoping for a change in this depressing situation, we should not demand our new managers to purge: firing the old “scoundrels” and hiring new “honest” individuals. On the contrary, we should demand the creation of conditions where it is disadvantageous for these same people to steal, kill, and accept bribes.

Then those people who used to work in the Security Service, customs, and executive committee will go into business, production, and agriculture. As the most energetic and successful individuals, they will quickly figure out where they can earn money faster and will be the first ones there.

One should not rely on honest and naive individuals who act from pure heart, but on cunning, sly, and selfish ones who are tightly bound by the law. If the authority acts in this way, by changing laws and methods of control without changing people, it can be trusted. Otherwise, if the authority purges its yesterday’s comrades, can we trust it?

Put a wolf to guard the sheep flock. When it kills the first sheep, kill it as a sign of lost trust. And invite a new wolf. This will be the illustration.
