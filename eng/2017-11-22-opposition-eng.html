Recently, an acquaintance asked me if I was opposed to the government. The question made me think, and soon I found an answer that surprised both my interlocutor and myself. I am not opposed to the government - I am opposed to my own people. I see not the government, but our own Ukrainian people as the main enemy. I am in relentless opposition to them. I consider them to be the cause of all troubles and believe that they urgently need to be purged.

In my strong belief, the main political conflict in Ukraine is not a power struggle with the opposition, but a conflict between the intelligent and the fools. It is evident that the number of the latter is rapidly increasing.

Both in power and in opposition, there have been wealthy and smart people for many years who successfully exploit a foolish and obedient population. The problem is not with them. They are not the root of evil, they are only its consequence.

Evil is gullibility, greed, political illiteracy, ignorance, thievery, servility, and citizen irresponsibility. Such a people rightly have the power and opposition they have. Ukrainian society does not need parliamentary opposition in power, but a nationwide opposition to fools.

Here are four categories of “fools” that, in my opinion, are the most dangerous and should be fought against as a priority at all levels and whenever possible:

“Grechkoedy” is the term used to describe those who vote in elections, figuratively speaking, for a package of buckwheat. At first glance, these people can be understood - they are far from politics, rightfully distrustful that one candidate-criminal is better than another candidate-cop, and have long been disillusioned with any government, and take money for their votes. This behavior, which is momentarily justified but strategically destructive, turns the entire population into easily manipulated herds, which various Korban and Filatov individuals successfully take advantage of. By selling their voting rights, “grechkoedy” turn democracy into “grechkocracy,” and the people’s power into the power of stolen money. How to defeat the “grechkoedy”? It is not the oligarchs in parliament, who are inaccessible, but the people who live next to us and enslave us and push us into poverty with their apathy, greed, and stupidity. To fight them, there is no need to set tires on fire and destroy tents on Maidan. It is enough to simply explain the seriousness and danger of their behavior.

Patriots. Neither common sense, nor expediency, nor logic, nor sober reasoning, nor plans, nor arguments of reason can extinguish the flames of righteous patriotic xenophobia. Patriots seek enemies, not reasons. Patriots dream of victory “over,” not “for.” Patriots need a reason to hate, not reasons to love. As Oscar Wilde said, “patriotism is the virtue of the vicious.” Patriots cover vices with bright flags, emblems, anthems, and illustrated books about the rich history of “their” people. Patriotism does not know how to think, it knows how to act. To act under the guidance of those who think. It is necessary to fight against patriotism at all levels, from the domestic and beyond. Although, above, it hardly exists at all. It thrives at the bottom, where long reflections are not honored, but bright flags are.

Activists. At the beginning of the last century, in the Soviet labor camp system GULAG, there was a category of prisoners who collaborated with the administration. They were called “activists.” They helped the administration maintain order and discipline among the prisoners. Ukraine in its current state, with its impoverished people living practically in slavery, is not much different from a Stalinist concentration camp. Various activists, urging us to clean up yards and roadsides, raising funds to help children, environmentalists, and fighters for European values, are playing into the hands of the ruling corrupt class, distracting the population from the real problem. It is not sick children that need to be saved, but officials from the healthcare system who need to be imprisoned. We should not be collecting funds for a leukemia fight fund, but investigating the multimillion-dollar embezzlement from the country’s budget. You can’t cure a cancerous tumor with Zelenka (a common antiseptic), and that’s exactly the kind of treatment that “activists” of all kinds propose. I urge you to take a closer look at the fighters for “good at any cost” - they are traitors.

Bribe lovers. “He doesn’t take bribes, he is thanked by others” - such a description of an “honest” official can be heard very often, in a very positive context. Bribery is such a common and familiar phenomenon for us that many simply do not realize that it is a crime. We have committees to fight corruption, slogans and high-profile criminal cases, but we still consider a judge in a Mercedes and the head of the regional administration in a Lexus as “respected and decent” people, because they are not bribe-takers or corrupt officials, people are simply “grateful” to them. Our tolerant attitude towards bribes at the everyday level is the foundation of systemic corruption in Ukraine. Let’s change our attitude towards expensive suits and watches of people in power - they are thieves, not “respected” individuals.

Recently, I was asked what exactly we, specifically you and I, can do to change the situation in our impoverished and devastated Ukraine. I believe that we need to start by fighting against these four categories of our enemies. Fighting at our level, in everyday communication, on social media, in offices and at home.

These people are the root of our nation’s troubles.
