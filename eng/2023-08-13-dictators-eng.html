According to historians, empires, emperors, and slavery existed first. Then the empires fell because they became ashamed, and the dark ages of medieval feudalism began, which eventually bored all enlightened people, and under the cries of the newly discovered Native Americans, the New Era arrived, followed by the Renaissance. Slavery was abolished, although not immediately, and democracy was also implemented gradually. All former slaves were granted equal rights, with laws and constitutions being created for them. Kings and emperors were either hanged or renamed as “constitutional,” thus freeing individuals from the oppression of others, but it was a mistake…

The main achievement of many years of our struggle for freedom is the recognition of the right of every individual to have their own beliefs. What prevented them from having them before the victory of liberalism? Not what, but who. Other individuals hindered the freedom-loving individual, who unsurprisingly also had their own convictions. From the king to the landowner, everyone had their own opinion, which the serf had to take into account. And not just take into account, but obey someone else’s will, sometimes under the threat of death. It’s good that all of this is already in the distant past.

Liberal reforms depersonalized power, replacing the owner with parliament, and his good or bad will with laws. In dark times, runaway peasants were returned to the feudal lord, who both executed and showed mercy. In the present day of triumphant democracy, border violators are caught by customs control services and punished by the penal system. Interestingly, we are satisfied with the power of laws, while the power of a specific individual causes rejection. We are much more willing to be slaves to the constitution, the police, and society, rather than to an individual. We have replaced the power of specific authorities with the authority of nameless power.

The United States, as the guardian of liberalism, has been attacking its opponents with the same weapon for many decades: “the lack of power rotation indicates limitations on freedom!” It doesn’t matter how a dictator behaves - their long-lasting presence in power poses a threat to the liberal world order. No matter what freedoms they bestow upon their subjects, no matter how successful the economy is, no matter how just their will may be, and no matter how much support they enjoy from their people - they are usurpers and medieval tyrants. A freedom-loving nation is obliged to overthrow them and establish a power of impersonal laws and parliaments.

The civilized world takes pride in the alternation of power. Presidential and parliamentary elections, capturing the attention of the entire population of a country, regularly remind us that the person in power will be replaced in the near future. Why will they be replaced? Because they failed? Because they turned out to be deceivers? Because they didn’t live up to our trust? No! They will be replaced because we do not recognize anyone’s authority over us, except the authority of laws. We do not acknowledge the power and control of another individual over us. Each of us is a unique and inviolable person, with our own inner world, beliefs, opinions, and understanding of life. We may tolerate another talker in a suit behind the podium for some time, but to acknowledge that they are the determiner of our destinies - never!

To further strengthen our belief in our victory over the forces of despotism, we are shown the insignificance and vulnerability of people in power. Corrupt and sexual scandals surrounding US presidents no longer surprise us; it’s time to put one of them behind bars. It’s so democratic that presidents know how to fall in love, get divorced, and take bribes. Not like the kings of the time of the Louis, about whom the commoners only knew that they were chosen by God and would execute their subjects in the most cruel ways at any convenient opportunity.

We, ordinary people, have won! Power belongs to us! The kings are overthrown! Only clowns remain (sometimes quite literally, think of Reagan and Zelensky), from whom little depends! Everything depends on us, on our will, on our voting voice!

Neither power nor resources can be depersonalized, nor can they belong to everyone at once. A human being is not only a social creature but also a herd animal. It is inherent in us to desire to form hierarchies and to submit to leaders. The apparent absence of dictators and hierarchies of power personally subordinate to them only indicates that their existence is carefully hidden. Both power and resources have long been distributed among those who realized in time that the profession of a king had become unsafe and retreated into corporate shadow, leaving well-paid sycophants on the podiums.

Masking themselves as actor-politicians and controlling resources through corporations, the people in real power act in their own interests rather than those of the people who unwittingly entrusted them with power and resources. Can we, the people, control them if we don’t even know the names of these plutocrats? The names of “bloody” dictators are known to us. Even though they make mistakes and usurp power, at least their own conscience and sense of responsibility towards the people control them. But who or what controls the oligarchs?

Returning to the reasons: our liberal rejection of “royal authority” has led us to the power of oligarchy. We are bribed with promises of “freedom and equality,” but in the end, we are sold democracy in its most repulsive forms: with fake politicians and absolute irresponsibility of the ruling elite.
