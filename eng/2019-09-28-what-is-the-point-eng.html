You refuse to be dull with us, and we refuse to support you. However, it hasn’t always been this way.

Previous generations witnessed an open war between two ideologies. The newspaper “Pravda” told the truth, while the magazine Time lied. And vice versa, depending on who was reading. It doesn’t matter who was actually right. What matters is that objectively the bipolar agenda gathered the intellectual elite around it. Where else to gather, if the world is polarized, and of course, it is interesting to talk and think about it?! Limonov’s first books were written from emigration, where he ended up after a conflict with the KGB - “either we imprison you or you leave” - that’s how it was formulated, according to his own words.

The vector of social conflict (capitalism versus communism) coincided with the vector of the most progressive thought (freedom or slavery). The best people were either in the academy of sciences or in exile. And in both places, there were geniuses, heated debates, and the opportunity to find the truth.

However, the ideology of Lenin and Stalin has lost. Limonov returned to Russia, back to where the war had already ended. There were no longer two camps, no dissidents, and no KGB - the fire of global revolution had died down. The intellectual elite returned from exile and celebrated victory, while the economy surrendered to the mercy of the victors - they brought us McDonald’s and Casio calculators in exchange for oil and a hungry, illiterate market of 150 million people. We emerged from the gloomy interlude of the 1990s without a new political agenda. The Soviet Union, once considered the “evil empire,” turned into a handful of underdeveloped colonies that no longer dream of fighting against the yoke of capital, and people became weaker and more cowardly. There was no longer a need to seek truth - just work for Uncle Sam and be happy.

It took me a few minutes to come up with a concise response, different from the primitive “ours” or “not ours.”

The world has become more complex, simple formulas like “ours vs. yours” or “freedom vs. slavery” are no longer able to describe the reality surrounding us. Anyone who wants to present a situation in terms of “for” or “against” is not just foolish. They are harmful. Whether they are liberals or Kremlin propagandists.

Why am I writing this blog? To instill in you, my readers, a love for forms with open fields. If we can fill in open fields, then there will be true freedom.
