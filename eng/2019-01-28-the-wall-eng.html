The United States has been fighting illegal immigration for a long time. Construction of a fence between California and Mexico began as early as 1993. In 2006, George Bush added an additional 900 kilometers of reinforcement to it, and more recently, Donald Trump has joined the fight. Observing his activity on Twitter and Instagram, one gets the impression that for the past few months, all of America has been concerned with one thing: how to protect themselves from the invasion of Mexicans. Are there no other problems? Why is this wall so important? Why now? Is it really so critical that the government had to be shut down for a whole month?

What task is Trump and his supporters trying to solve by focusing Americans’ attention on this issue and inevitably attracting huge negative attention from those who do not support the idea of such a strict and radical isolation of the US territory from the external, even though unreliable, world? Why risk his reputation and enter into conflict with a significant portion of American society? Judging by the statistics, illegal immigration is already decreasing, even without building a wall.

Maybe Trump wants to go down in history as the builder of the great Mexican wall? After all, he is a builder by trade, with buildings bearing his name, such as in New York and Las Vegas. Now there will also be the Trump Wall. In other words, maybe it’s his personal ambition. This version doesn’t seem plausible, though, as Trump has the support of the Republican Party and significant American money. They wouldn’t support him if it were just a project to immortalize his name. He’s not Gaddafi or Lukashenko, after all.

Perhaps the answer lies within the question itself: why do we need a wall if it generates such intense protest, yet fails to address the actual problem, especially when there isn’t a significant issue to begin with?

Maybe that’s exactly why it’s needed? Maybe it’s needed by Trump and those behind him not to separate America from Mexico, but specifically to divide America into two warring camps? Those who support xenophobic policies (Republicans) and those who are categorically against it (Democrats). In other words, to polarize society.

It doesn’t really matter what serves as a reason for this: whether it’s Mexican drug traffickers, which need to be immediately fought against, or global warming, which requires all efforts to be put into saving, or something else. It doesn’t matter. The important thing is to find a reason that will unequivocally divide the people into left and right, those who are “for” and those who are “against”, regardless of the subject matter. And once the reason is found, a spokesperson needs to be found, someone who is loud enough, convinced, and determined enough to make half of the people follow him, while the other half opposes him. America has found such a hero.

Why is polarization necessary? It makes it easier to control the people. It is easier to impose one’s political will on them if every citizen is firmly aligned with a particular camp. The average voter should know exactly who they are willing to vote for. They should not have any doubts. They should know who their friends and enemies are. They should be loyal to their party, their leader, and despise the opposing party and its leader.

This wall is being built not between the USA and Mexico.

This wall is being built between us, ordinary voters.

Do we need it? Do we need polarization? Similar processes are taking place in Ukraine and Russia, but instead of Mexican drug dealers, they are fueled by Bandera supporters from Uzhhorod, child killers from Donetsk, and Putin the cannibal. The essence is the same: it is necessary to divide voters into two camps and then act according to the situation, directing each camp in the desired direction.
