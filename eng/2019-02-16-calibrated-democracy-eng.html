“Калиброванная демократия”

Democracy is the worst form of government. Power belongs to the crowd, which is incapable of thinking strategically and consists mostly of unintelligent people who make impulsive decisions. This is what Aristotle believed in the 2nd century BC. He considered a more effective system of government to be one in which power belongs to those who have a greater authority to possess it, and therefore make more balanced and knowledgeable decisions.

Contrary to the thoughts of the Greek philosopher, democracy is currently the prevailing form of government in most countries around the world. Even in countries where monarchy is preserved, such as the United Kingdom or Spain, the actual governance is carried out by the cabinet of ministers, and laws are passed by the democratically elected parliament. On paper, it is the people through their representatives who govern all countries in the world, including Cuba, North Korea, and China.

For example, Article 71 of the Constitution of Ukraine states that “elections to the bodies of state power and bodies of local self-government are free and are conducted on the basis of universal, equal, and direct suffrage by secret ballot.”

Aristotle, and much later Vilfredo Pareto, Gaetano Mosca, and many other proponents of elitism, were precisely against this equality. They believed that people are inherently unequal in many ways, both physically and intellectually. And, more importantly, there are significantly more weak and foolish individuals in any society than strong and intelligent ones. Thus, by granting equal rights to everyone and giving the “green light” to decisions made by the simple majority, we inevitably create conditions for the degradation of the entire community. The most rational and strategically balanced decisions will be in the minority alongside their authors, simply due to inherent inequality in human abilities. Crudely speaking, fools will always shout down the wise.

To solve the problem, it is logical to either a) eliminate fools altogether, b) deprive them of the right to vote, or c) make them believe they have the right to vote when in fact they do not. The Nazis attempted to pursue the first path, but were stopped in time. The second path leads to massive revolutions, as exemplified by the events of 1917. Currently, all developed countries, led by the United States, are following the third path - creating an illusion of democracy where every citizen has equal voting rights, but the actual governance of the country is carried out regardless of the majority’s choice.

In other words, modern democracy is a qualitative spectacle staged by a minority for the majority in order to appease them and instill in them a sense of universal equality. Note once again, all of this is done primarily in the interests of the majority.

Может быть есть четвертый путь?

Есть.

What if we replace equal voting rights with unequal ones? What if every citizen doesn’t have just one vote that they “cast” into the ballot box every few years, but several, depending on their value to society, contribution to its development, and authority. Let’s say an economics professor would have 50 votes, while a hair stylist would have only two.

Such a “calibrated” democracy will solve two problems at once. Firstly, it will bring to power those who deserve it and whose opinion we all should really rely on. Secondly, it will explain to everyone else what exactly they lack in order to also become those whose opinion the whole country listens to. At the same time, this will be done openly, objectively, and fairly, minimizing the eternal conflict between the rich and the poor, the smart and the foolish, the bottom and the top of any society.

Most likely, this model will not lead to a change in people in power. However, it will make the electoral formula significantly more understandable and fair. After all, it is these qualities that modern democracy lacks.
